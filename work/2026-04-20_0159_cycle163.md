# Weaver Integration Map — Verified Connections & Integration Gaps  
**Cycle:** 163  
**Phase:** 4 — Build & Test  
**Status:** Mapping current connections and identifying integration points.

Based on reading the actual files in the fleet repos, here is the current state of integration between **plato-torch**, **fleet-simulator**, **holodeck-rust**, and **plato-ensign**.

---

## 1. **plato-torch** (Training Room Engine)
**Role:** Core training environment. Hosts 25 preset rooms, including `DeadbandRoom`. Manages tile ingestion, agent interaction, and room state.

**Current Connections:**
- **Connected to plato-ensign:** Yes — direct export path.
  - `plato-torch/src/rooms/deadband_room.py` includes `export_to_ensign()` method.
  - Exports room experience (tiles, interactions) as LoRA training data.
- **Connected to fleet-simulator:** Indirect — via shared tile format.
  - Both consume/produce `.tile` files in `memory/tiles/`.
  - No direct API calls observed.
- **Connected to holodeck-rust:** No direct connection found.
  - Separate tech stacks (Python vs Rust).
  - No import statements or cross-references in code.

**Integration Points:**
- `memory/tiles/` directory acts as a shared filesystem bus.
- Room presets define tile schemas that other tools could adopt.

---

## 2. **fleet-simulator** (Multi-Agent Simulation)
**Role:** Generates training data via simulated fleet interactions.

**Current Connections:**
- **Connected to plato-torch:** Indirect — tile sharing.
  - Simulator writes `.tile` files to `memory/tiles/`.
  - plato-torch rooms can read these as training data.
- **Connected to holodeck-rust:** No connection found.
  - Different domains (simulation vs. interactive MUD).
- **Connected to plato-ensign:** No direct connection.
  - Simulator output could be converted to ensign format, but no observed pipeline.

**Integration Points:**
- Tile format alignment enables data flow.
- Simulator could feed `DeadbandRoom` or other training rooms.

---

## 3. **holodeck-rust** (Sentiment-Aware MUD Server)
**Role:** Interactive multi-user dungeon with NPC sentiment systems.

**Current Connections:**
- **Connected to plato-torch:** None.
  - Separate language, runtime, and purpose.
- **Connected to fleet-simulator:** None.
- **Connected to plato-ensign:** None.

**Integration Gaps:**
- No mechanism to inject ghost tiles or training data.
- NPC sentiment engine could benefit from plato-torch room training.
- Standalone service; not wired into the fleet training loop.

---

## 4. **plato-ensign** (LoRA Adapter Exporter)
**Role:** Exports room experience from plato-torch into lightweight LoRA adapters.

**Current Connections:**
- **Connected to plato-torch:** Yes — primary input.
  - Direct API call: `room.export_to_ensign()`.
  - Reads tile history and interaction logs.
- **Connected to fleet-simulator:** None.
- **Connected to holodeck-rust:** None.

**Integration Points:**
- Could be extended to consume simulator tiles or holodeck logs.
- Output adapters could be loaded into runtime agents (cudaclaw, flux-runtime).

---

## **Summary: What’s Connected**
1. **plato-torch ↔ plato-ensign** — Direct export pipeline.
2. **plato-torch ↔ fleet-simulator** — Shared tile storage (filesystem bus).
3. **fleet-simulator → memory/tiles/** — Data generation for training.

## **What’s Not Connected**
1. **holodeck-rust** — Isolated. No links to other components.
2. **fleet-simulator → plato-ensign** — No direct conversion.
3. **holodeck-rust → training loop** — No ghost tile injection or sentiment training export.
