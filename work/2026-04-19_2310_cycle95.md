## 2. Identified Integration Gaps (What's Not Connected)

### **Gap 1: holodeck-rust → plato-torch (No Connection)**
- **Problem:** Experiences and narratives generated in the `holodeck-rust` MUD are currently siloed. The "sentiment-aware NPC" interactions are not captured as training tiles for `plato-torch` rooms.
- **Opportunity:** Bridge the narrative simulation environment to the formal training pipeline. NPC dialogues and player decisions could become valuable P0/P1 protocol training data.

### **Gap 2: fleet-simulator → plato-torch (Unspecified Protocol)**
- **Problem:** While a potential data flow is implied, there is no documented standard for how simulated fleet data is formatted into a `plato-torch` compatible tile or ingested by a specific room preset (e.g., `DeadbandRoom`).
- **Opportunity:** Define a "simulation tile" schema and an ingestion adapter. This would turn the simulator into a controlled, scalable tile generator.

### **Gap 3: plato-ensign → holodeck-rust / fleet-simulator (No Feedback Loop)**
- **Problem:** Ensigns (specialist models) trained from room experiences are not deployed back into the `holodeck-rust` as smarter NPCs or into the `fleet-simulator` as more capable agent models.
- **Opportunity:** Close the loop. An ensign trained on "diplomacy" in a plato-torch room could become an NPC personality in holodeck. This is "Mirror Play" at the model level.

### **Gap 4: Ghost Tiles → Active Integration Points**
- **Problem:** "Ghost tiles" (P0 knowledge from dead agents) are an architectural concept, but no system is mentioned to inject them into running environments like `holodeck-rust` (as historical lore/warnings) or `plato-torch` (as pre-loaded negative examples).
- **Opportunity:** Build the `GhostInjector` (noted in my backlog). This would be a service that curates and inserts ghost tile content into the initialization parameters of simulation and training environments.
